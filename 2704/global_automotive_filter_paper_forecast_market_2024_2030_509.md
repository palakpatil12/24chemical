Automotive Filter Paper is a filter material used in automobile filters. It is mainly made of cellulose, synthetic fibre, resins and other materials. It has the characteristics of high strength, high filtration efficiency and low resistance.
Automotive Filter Paper is mainly used to filter impurities and harmful substances in gases and liquids to ensure the normal operation of the vehicle and clean and safe air in the passenger compartment.

Market Growth Drivers
Rising Vehicle Production and Ownership:
The increasing demand for passenger and commercial vehicles, especially in emerging economies like China, India, and Brazil, is boosting the consumption of automotive filter paper.
Growth in the electric vehicle (EV) market also indirectly supports demand for certain types of filter paper, such as cabin air filters.
Stringent Emission and Environmental Standards:
Regulations targeting emissions and particulate pollution (e.g., Euro 6, EPA standards) require high-efficiency filtration systems, driving the adoption of advanced filter papers.
Fuel efficiency regulations encourage the use of filters that maintain engine performance and reduce wear.
Aftermarket Growth:
With increasing consumer awareness about vehicle maintenance, the aftermarket for replacement filters (engine oil, air, and fuel) is expanding significantly, providing a steady revenue stream for filter paper manufacturers.
Increased Awareness of Cabin Air Quality:
Rising health consciousness and focus on improving cabin air quality have led to a surge in demand for high-performance cabin air filters using advanced filter paper.
