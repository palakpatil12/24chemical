</p><p><strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Polyester polyols are a category of polyols synthesized through the condensation reaction of dibasic acids (such as adipic or phthalic acid) with polyhydric alcohols (like ethylene glycol or glycerol). These materials are a core component in the formulation of polyurethanes, imparting essential characteristics such as flexibility, chemical resistance, and durability. Their applications span across a wide range of industries including automotive, construction, electronics, and consumer goods, particularly where mechanical performance and chemical resilience are critical.</p><p>
